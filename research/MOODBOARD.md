# Quirky Moodboard

> Phase 3 deliverable. Date: 2026-05-31. Author: 3mpq-researcher.
> Emotional target: a delightful, soft, toy-like FRIEND that happens to be a precise
> developer tool. Warm, rounded, confident, never cutesy-babyish, never corporate-cold.

---

## Emotional ambition

The feeling on first scroll should be: "oh, this is charming, and it is also exactly
the tool I needed." Relief plus a small grin. The page should feel like a well-made
physical toy: rounded, satisfying, a little playful to touch, but precision-engineered
inside. Think a beautifully-machined fidget object, not a cartoon.

## Accent — LOCKED (see product-truth.md, full detail in quirky-direction.md)

LOCKED: **warm coral `#FF7059`** (hover `#F25742`, soft `#FFE9E4`, pressed `#DB4733`,
subtle `#FFF4F1`). One accent rule. Banned: forest green, any `#217a50`, rec-red
`#b7443d`. Coral serves the FRIEND mandate hardest and is on the 2025-2026 warm-coral
trend line. Considered-but-not-chosen: soft violet `#7C5CFC`, soft cerulean `#3D9DF2`.

All on a warm off-white base `#FDFCFA`, warm border `#ECEAE6`, near-black fg `#1A1A1A`.

## Visual language

- **Shape**: soft blobs + squircles (22.4% superellipse), generous radii. One blob
  object per mode (OCR/HEX/DOM/SVG/SPX) plus utility blobs (capture rectangle, Tab
  switch, copied-checkmark). All single-accent + neutral, one stroke language.
- **Type**: Manrope throughout (variable). Big friendly display weights for headlines,
  comfortable 16px+ body. No serif (this is a clean break from Corder's IBM Plex Serif).
  Mono only inside code/selector chips.
- **Surface**: warm paper white, hairline borders carry the layout, soft optional dot/
  blob field for atmosphere on hero and FinalCTA. Shadows minimal and soft, light-theme
  only.
- **Motion**: pneumatic only. BlurReveal entries, native scroll-timeline fills, the
  five mode-chips "deal/fan" out of one capture. Nothing bounces.

## Reference set (NOT Corder)

1. **Arc Browser (arc.net / The Browser Company, pre-rebrand era)** — the north star
   for "playful but premium" software marketing: soft, warm, confident, human. Pull:
   the balance of delight and credibility, the rounded warmth.
2. **Raycast (raycast.com)** — menu-bar utility premium-vibe and "one entry point, many
   powers" compositional clarity. Pull the clarity; invert the theme (warm-light, not
   dark) and the temperature (friendly, not cool).
3. **Linear (linear.app)** — discipline benchmark: typography, hairline grids, restrained
   scroll motion. Pull the rigor; pour warmth and blobs over it.
4. **Sip (sipapp.io)** — closest competitor in MOOD: designerly warmth around a precise
   tool. Pull the tasteful, calm craft.
5. **Card "fan/deal" hero motion** (fintech card landings, 2024-2026) — the mechanic for
   fanning five mode-chips out of one capture rectangle.

## Texture / supplementary

- Warm paper-grain backgrounds (ia.net restraint level, not heavy).
- A small, consistent illustrated blob set (the kind seen across friendly 2024-2026
  SaaS landings), but bespoke and single-accent for ownership.
- Real product micro-recordings (`.webm`) for the 5 mode demos, per research-prompt,
  not Lottie-over-screenshots.

## Anti-references (do NOT look like these)

- Corder (rec-red, IBM Plex Serif, recording-window hero, quiet-engineer voice).
- Multicolor playful brands (ColorSlurp): violates one-accent doctrine.
- Cold corporate SaaS (gradient mesh + stock photos + "enterprise" language).
- Bouncy/elastic toy sites: friendliness without rigor reads cheap.
- AI-illustrated heros and stock photos of people in headphones (banned).

## One-line mood statement

A precision Mac utility wearing the soft, rounded, single-coral skin of a well-made
toy: warm paper, blobby mode-objects, Manrope, pneumatic motion, and the quiet
confidence of "point at it, get the data."
